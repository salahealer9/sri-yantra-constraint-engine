# Study A — random-sample landscape estimate (frozen)

Companion to `draw.txt` and `results.csv`. **Exploratory** (planning only, off the
confirmatory track per Amendment-02 §B0). Generated by `../study_a.py`.

- Seed **20260614**; population **815** well-posed plane subsets (degenerate
  {1,2,8,9,16} excluded); sample **N = 40** (the prefix of the seed's shuffle —
  extensible without bias).
- Float AA (box count is float==rounded, calibration width ratio 1.0000, so counts
  transfer to the rigorous campaign). Caps: r_cert 3e-3, max_boxes 1e6, tlim 600 s.
  Box = illustrative `route3_panel.BOX`, not the registered pre-registration box.

## Headline

**40 / 40 completed; 0 right-censored.** No subset exhausted the budget — the
first and most important result, since execution risk was the open question.

| | |
|---|---|
| complete & feasible (≥1 certified figure) | **19 / 40** |
| complete & infeasible (certified absence) | **21 / 40** |
| feasible fraction | **48 %** (Wilson 95% CI ≈ ±15 pp) |

## Box-count distribution (transfers to the rigorous campaign)

| stat | boxes |

**No order-of-magnitude tail.** The largest subset is 2.23× the median — a tight,
predictable distribution, not the heavy tail we braced for.

Five largest (subset, boxes, max depth, feasible):

| subset | boxes | depth | feasible |

**Big subsets are wide, not deep:** the largest sit at moderate depth (24–27) with
large box counts — broad undecided shells near the variety, not deep tangencies.
Six of the eight largest are *infeasible*: certifying absence over the whole box
tends to cost more than finding a figure and excluding the rest.

## Memory (DFS-bounded — confirmed)

Peak queue **30**, max subdivision depth **50**, across all 40. No memory hazard.

## Hazard hypotheses — falsified

| predictor | prediction | result |
|---|---|---|
| radial-dependency proximity | above-median cost | not supported (calibration probes ordinary) |
| F20 (c=d symmetry) | above-median depth/cost | **falsified**: F20-bearing (n=4) mean depth 31.5 / 71k boxes vs non-F20 32.2 / 83k |

Only two depth outliers exist — {1,2,4,16,20} (depth 50) and {1,2,8,14,19}
(depth 45) — both at ordinary box counts, both feasible. **No working structural
predictor of cost remains.** Cost variation is real (~3× spread) but not explained
by the structural features guessed so far. This is a constraint on any future
Study-B hazard scan: there is no obvious target to aim it at.

## Campaign extrapolation (rough)

Mean 82k × 815 ≈ **67 M boxes**. At rigorous ~800 box/s: ~23 h single-core,
**~5.8 h on 4 cores**; faster in Julia. The plane campaign is a day-job, not a
research risk.

## Caveats / what is not established

- n = 40 of 815 (~5 %): CIs are wide; a pathology rarer than ~1/40 would not be
  caught. "No tail" means "no tail at the ~1-in-40 detection threshold."
- Box counts at r_cert = 3e-3 over the illustrative box; absolute counts shift with
  r_cert and the registered box (rigor ratios ~1 hold).
- Float run; rigorous arithmetic adds ~1.6× per-box (Python), recoverable in Julia.
- Spherical form (3044 subsets, 6-D, heavier) not studied; secondary per the prereg.
