## Email body

Dear Editor,

I am writing regarding C. S. Rao's paper *Śrīyantra — A Study of Spherical and Plane Forms*, published in this journal (Indian Journal of History of Science, **33**(3), 1998, 203–227).

As part of an independent study, I reimplemented Rao's full construction and his system of twenty geometric constraint functions in order to reproduce the solutions tabulated in the paper. The formulation is sound and its tabulated results are reproducible; in the course of this work, however, I identified four transcription errors in specific printed equations (eqs 2.22, 3.3, 3.4, and 3.14b), together with one plane-reduction correction (eq 6.12). With these corrections applied, the affected constraints vanish at Rao's own tabulated solutions to within numerical precision. Each correction was verified two independent ways: by reproduction against the published solution tables, and by an independent reconstruction of the figure in coordinate geometry. To my knowledge these corrections have not been reported previously.

I would like to offer a short corrigendum note documenting these corrections, with their derivation and the supporting evidence, for the journal's consideration. The verification code and a complete write-up are openly archived with a DOI (Zenodo: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20617730), so every result is independently reproducible.

The journal is welcome to involve the original author, or any appropriate reviewer, in considering this note. My aim is simply to improve the accuracy of the published record of a paper I have found valuable and built upon. Please let me know the form in which the journal would prefer to receive such a note, and whether the Editorial Manager portal or direct email is the appropriate channel.

Thank you for your time and for the journal's work.
