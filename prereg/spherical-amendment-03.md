# spherical-amendment-03 — Preregistered hypothesis H4 (F₇ enrichment)

- **Filed:** _to be GPG-signed and OpenTimestamps-stamped before the size-six census is executed._
- **Amends:** spherical preregistration line (v1.0.2 + Amendments 01–02).
- **Nature:** preregistration of a single confirmatory hypothesis for an independent census, frozen before execution.

## Purpose

Exploratory observation EO-1 (F₇ appears in 32 of the 76 robust curvature-confined
size-five subsets) was generated by the size-five census and is therefore in-sample
to it. This amendment preregisters one confirmatory test of F₇ enrichment on the
**independent size-six census population**, so that the test is genuinely
out-of-sample. Its only purpose is to fix that hypothesis before the size-six data
are seen; once frozen, its job is done.

## Scope

Exactly one confirmatory hypothesis is preregistered (H4 below). No mechanism,
conditioning, geometric, or other exploratory analysis is included. The
branch-level observations from the size-five census (boundary-mechanism dominance,
fold prevalence) describe altitude *branches*; the size-six systems are isolated
solutions, not branches, so those observables are not testable out-of-sample. They
remain descriptive in the size-five manuscript and are **not** promoted to
confirmatory hypotheses here. Any F₇ analysis beyond H4 is exploratory.

## Hypothesis H4 (F₇ enrichment)

> Subsets containing F₇ are enriched among robust-realizable size-six spherical
> systems, relative to subsets not containing F₇.

- **Population.** The well-posed size-six determined subsets ({F₁,F₂} together with
  four of {F₃,…,F₂₀}, less rank-deficient selections; N ≈ 3044, fixed by the same
  well-posedness criterion used for the size-five census). This population is distinct 
  from the 815 size-five subsets that generated EO-1 and was not used in generating the 
  hypothesis.
- **Predictor.** F₇ ∈ subset (binary).
- **Outcome (robust-realizable).** A subset is robust-realizable iff at least one
  Gate-4-valid spherical solution is found that is **not near-degeneracy-flagged**
  (passes the τ_deg = 10⁻³ base-point-gap floor frozen in the size-five
  preregistration); otherwise it is not robust-realizable. The near-zero-width
  (tangency) flag is a branch property and does not apply, since size-six altitude
  is determined and the solutions are isolated.
- **Test.** One-sided Fisher's exact test on the 2×2 table
  [F₇ present / absent] × [robust-realizable / not], with alternative H₁: odds
  ratio > 1.
- **Effect estimate.** Odds ratio with 95% confidence interval.
- **Multiplicity.** H4 is the sole confirmatory test; no correction is applied.
- **Significance threshold.** α = 0.05, one-sided.
- **Decision rule.** H4 is supported iff p < 0.05 and the odds ratio exceeds 1;
  otherwise H4 is not supported.

## Execution

The size-six census will be classified by the same frozen engine and procedure
(engine SHA-256 `a0772717e4d07c327e744608bd0abf4f9a50d5f343b07fc3ffc119a7cd8a59af`; 
spherical preregistration v1.0.2 + Amendments 01--02): identical stable seeding, 
near-pole grid, completeness audit, and Gate-4 validity and near-degeneracy 
definitions. No inspection of F₇ enrichment statistics from the size-six census 
will be performed prior to execution of the preregistered test. The 2$\times$2 table, 
test, odds ratio, confidence interval, and decision are computed exactly as specified 
above, with no post-hoc modification.
